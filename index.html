About Marilyn

I'm Marilyn, a fourth-year computer science student at UC Berkeley. I studied economics at first, then switched to data science, before facing my fear of coding and switching to computer science head-on. I've struggled a lot at Berkeley with the battle of pursuing something completely different from software engineering vs feeling like I had to prove myself and my skills in computer science. At a school like Berkeley, it has been hard to pursue my creative side fully because many people value technical skills on top of all, which I have trouble relating to. It's still a debate I have today, but I do enjoy aspects of CS still.


Non CS-wise, for the past three years I have been in acting classes, working with filmmakers and directors, and filming short films on the side of school. Being on camera via acting, vlogging, reading my writing out loud on Tiktok or my old hobby of Twitch streaming has always felt so natural to me. With acting, I single-handedly believe empathy can travel from the actors, through the movie screen, to the audience. I want to make others feel something new, something they haven't really felt in their life situations before or let them face an emotion they haven't been able to process. I want to teach others empathy by sharing the stories and feelings of people in a myriad of life circumstances, both in real-life and by impact through my acting. My all-time favorite film is Interstellar, and current favorite film is No Hard Feelings.


Lastly, I'm also a musician and currently love making up edm accompaniments on piano. I also love watching basketball, running and lifting and playing sports, playing Brawl Stars and some PC games occasionally, and writing.
